# Beneficiary Directory

Manage foster youth enrollment and track participation across AFFCF programs.

## Who We Serve

**1,760 foster youth** in Arizona
- Ages 5-21
- Currently in foster care or recently aged out
- Residing in Maricopa, Pima, and surrounding counties

## Beneficiary Categories

### Active Participants
Currently enrolled in one or more programs:
- Regular attendance (>75%)
- Making progress toward goals
- Engaged in services

### Transitioning
Youth preparing to age out (18-21):
- Transition services enrollment
- Life skills development
- Housing/employment support
- College enrollment assistance

### Alumni
Former participants who:
- Completed programs
- Aged out successfully
- May return for support services

### Inactive
Temporarily not participating:
- Lost contact
- Moved out of area
- Program mismatch
- Re-engagement efforts underway

## Key Information Tracked

**Demographics:**
- Name, age, gender
- Current placement type
- School/education status
- Special needs or accommodations

**Program Enrollment:**
- Active programs
- Enrollment dates
- Attendance rates
- Progress toward goals

**Case Management:**
- Assigned caseworker
- Primary contact (foster parent, DCS worker)
- Emergency contacts
- Service plan and goals

**Outcomes:**
- Academic progress
- Skill development
- Behavioral improvements
- Transition readiness (ages 16+)

## Privacy & Confidentiality

**CRITICAL:** Foster care records are **highly confidential**

**Always:**
- Use secure systems for data
- First name + last initial only in public documents
- Get consent before sharing photos/stories
- Protect DCS case information
- Follow FERPA for education records

**Never:**
- Share full names publicly
- Post identifiable photos without consent
- Discuss cases in public spaces
- Email sensitive information unencrypted

## Best Practices

### Intake Process
1. Verify foster care status (DCS confirmation)
2. Complete intake assessment
3. Set individual goals with youth
4. Obtain guardian consent
5. Assign to appropriate program(s)
6. Introduce to staff and volunteers

### Ongoing Case Management
- **Weekly:** Check-in with program staff
- **Monthly:** Review progress toward goals
- **Quarterly:** Formal assessment and goal updates
- **Annually:** Comprehensive evaluation

### Documentation
- Keep accurate attendance records
- Document significant incidents
- Track progress toward goals
- Maintain current contact information

## Success Indicators

**Good Signs:**
- Regular attendance (>85%)
- Progress toward goals
- Positive relationships with staff
- Skill development visible
- Engaged in activities

**Concerns:**
- Frequent absences
- Behavioral incidents
- No goal progress
- Disengagement
- Placement instability
