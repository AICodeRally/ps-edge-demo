## Budget Health Indicators

**Good (Green):**
- Spending 70-80% with 70-80% of year complete
- On track with projections
- No significant variances

**Watch (Yellow):**
- Spending >85% or <60% of budget
- Variance from projections
- Monitor closely

**Concern (Red):**
- Spending >95% before year-end
- Significant under-spending (grant compliance risk)
- Major variances unexplained

## Monthly Budget Review

**Review Each Month:**
1. Actual spending vs budget
2. Variance analysis
3. Forecast year-end position
4. Reallocation needs
5. Grant compliance

**Red Flags:**
- Spending significantly ahead or behind schedule
- Repeated overspending in category
- Under-utilizing grant funds (risk of clawback)
- No budget owner monitoring

## Grant-Specific Budgets

**Restricted Funds:**
- Must be spent per grant agreement
- Track separately from general funds
- Report to funder quarterly
- No commingling with unrestricted

**Unrestricted Funds:**
- Flexible use based on needs
- Board-approved budget
- Can cover grant matches
- Essential for sustainability

## Best Practices

### Budget Monitoring
- **Weekly** - Review spending in real-time
- **Monthly** - Full variance analysis
- **Quarterly** - Forecast and adjust
- **Annually** - Next year planning

### Expense Management
1. Get quotes for large purchases
2. Track all expenses with receipts
3. Code to correct program/grant
4. Approve before spending
5. Reconcile monthly

### Grant Compliance
- Spend grant funds per agreement
- Track match requirements
- Report spending quarterly
- Request modifications if needed
- Plan for audit

## Tips for Financial Health

1. **Monitor weekly** - Don't wait for month-end surprises
2. **Reallocate early** - Move funds between programs if needed
3. **Spend restricted first** - Use grant funds within timeframe
4. **Plan for cash flow** - Grants pay in arrears
5. **Document everything** - Receipts, approvals, justifications
