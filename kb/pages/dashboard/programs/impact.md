# Impact Measurement

Measure program effectiveness and demonstrate impact to funders.

## Why Impact Matters

**For Funders:**
- Prove program effectiveness
- Justify grant renewals
- Support funding requests

**For Programs:**
- Improve service delivery
- Identify what works
- Celebrate successes

**For Youth:**
- Track progress toward goals
- Celebrate achievements
- Identify support needs

## Key Outcome Metrics

### Education Programs
- **Reading improvement** - Grade levels gained
- **Math achievement** - Test score increases
- **Attendance rate** - Program participation
- **Grade progression** - On-time advancement

**Current Results:**
- 89% improved 2+ grade levels (target: 75%)
- 92% attendance rate (target: 85%)

### Life Skills Programs
- Skill mastery assessments
- Independence readiness scores
- Job placement rates
- Housing stability

### Transition Services (Ages 18-21)
- **Housing** - Permanent placement rate (target: 65%)
- **Employment** - Full-time job placement
- **Education** - College enrollment (68% vs 60% target)
- **Life skills** - Independent living readiness

## Data Collection Best Practices

### Monthly
- Attendance tracking
- Progress check-ins
- Incident reports

### Quarterly
- Skills assessments
- Goal achievement review
- Beneficiary surveys

### Annually
- Comprehensive outcome evaluation
- Success story documentation
- Funder reporting

## Tips for Grant Reporting

**Funders Want to See:**
1. **Clear metrics** - Specific, measurable outcomes
2. **Comparison data** - Actual vs target, before vs after
3. **Stories + stats** - Numbers AND human impact
4. **Methodology** - How you collected data
5. **Challenges** - Honest assessment of barriers

**Example Impact Statement:**
"Through our Keys to Success tutoring program, 127 foster youth improved an average of 2.3 grade levels in reading (target: 2.0 levels). Maria's story exemplifies this impact - she went from a 4th grade reading level to 7th grade in just 6 months and now loves learning."
