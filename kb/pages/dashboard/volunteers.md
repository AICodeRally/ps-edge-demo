# Volunteer Management

Manage your volunteer roster and track contributions.

## Volunteer Lifecycle

### Recruitment
1. Application and interview
2. Background check (required for youth programs)
3. Reference verification
4. Orientation and training

### Onboarding
- Mission and values training
- Role-specific training (tutoring, mentoring, etc.)
- Safety protocols for working with foster youth
- Trauma-informed care basics

### Engagement
- Regular shift assignments
- Recognition and appreciation
- Skill development opportunities
- Community building

### Retention
- Quarterly recognition events
- Annual volunteer appreciation
- Leadership development
- Impact storytelling

## Working with Foster Youth

### Critical Requirements

**Background Checks:**
- FBI fingerprint clearance required
- Renewed every 3 years
- Valid before first shift with youth

**Training Required:**
- Trauma-informed care (2 hours)
- Mandated reporter training
- Confidentiality and FERPA compliance
- Safety protocols

### Best Practices
- **Consistency** - Same volunteers build trust
- **Boundaries** - Professional relationships
- **Patience** - Trauma affects behavior
- **Positivity** - Focus on strengths
- **Communication** - Document interactions

## Quick Actions

- **View roster** - See all active volunteers
- **Track hours** - Monitor contributions (Volunteers > Hours)
- **Schedule shifts** - Manage assignments (Volunteers > Schedule)
- **Filter volunteers** - By skills, availability, or status

## Tips for Success

1. **Match skills to needs** - Math tutors for tutoring, event planners for fundraisers
2. **Communicate clearly** - Set expectations upfront
3. **Show impact** - Share student success stories
4. **Flexible opportunities** - Weekday, weekend, remote options
5. **Build community** - Create connections between volunteers
